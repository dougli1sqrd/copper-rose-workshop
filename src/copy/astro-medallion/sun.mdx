
# [The Sun](#sun)

### First, A Note on the Geocentric Model

Obviously the Earth revolves around the Sun. But I've chosen Earth as the center frame of reference
for Astro Medallion, so all "planets" (Sun, Moon, etc) will appear to revolve around the Earth. 
This is how the planets look when we see them from Earth. Astronomers and Astrologers regularly use an 
Earth-centric coordinate system for tracking the planets, but it's not to say that the Earth is
in actuality at the center.

## Observations About the Sun

The sun is interesting because you can see the equinoxes and solstices as it goes around
the Earth. Since there are 12 months in the year, and the Sun fully "goes around" the Earth once per 
year, and there are 12 signs of the Zodiac, it means that the sun moves into a new sign once per month!

Every time the Sun changes signs, that's obviously a new zodiac sign! The Sun changes sign around
the 20th of every month. The equinox is the time in Fall or Spring when the amount of daylight equals
the amount of nighttime. The spring equinox is in March, starting with Aries at 0° 
[ecliptic longitude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecliptic_coordinate_system) - which is why Astro Medallion
has the top start with Aries. The Sun moves counter-clockwise around the sky throughout the year 
until it's June when we have the Summer Solstice in Cancer. The Fall equinox is in September (Libra), and 
the Winter Solstice is in December (Capricorn).
