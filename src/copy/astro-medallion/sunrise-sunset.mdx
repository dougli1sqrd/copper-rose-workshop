# [Horizon Lights and Sunrise/Sunset](#sunrise)

You can use Astro Medallion to notice when the sunrise and sunsets are happening.

The lit warm-white LEDs around the border represent the currently visible part of the sky 
from your position on earth based on the current time - called the "Horizon Lights". 
They sweep an angle of 180°.

As the Earth rotates the portion of the sky that you can see moves, and this is reflected in which
horizon lights are lit. They will rotate counter-clockwise. Any planets underneath the lit area will
be visible to you at that moment. There are 36 horizon lights, and since there are 24 hours in a day,
going from one light to the next takes 40 minutes, and sweeping across one astrological sign takes 2 hours.

As the eastern horizon sweeps across the sky, the horizon lights continue to increment counter-clockwise.
To see the sunrise on Astro Medallion, then, we can observe when the horizon lights pass where the Sun LED
is lit, meaning the part of the sky containing the sun is now visible.

Similarly for sunset as the trailing part of the horizon lights advances and passes the sun, that
means the real life horizon is passing the sun and it is disappearing from our field of view.

Keep in mind that each occupies 30° of the sky, and each horizon light represents 10° so the timing of the
sunrise and sunset according to Astro Medallion will only be approximate compared to the real sunrise/sunset time.
